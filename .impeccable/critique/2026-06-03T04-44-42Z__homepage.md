## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: The visible product has been meaningfully de-slopped since the last pass. The borrow tab no longer buries the list under a manager KPI strip, the user's name is pre-filled, and the field-staff happy path is genuinely short. The remaining slop is structural, not visual: the stylesheet still wears two named design systems at once ("GA Platform Design Tokens" and "MeetFlow Theme") with 897 `!important` declarations and 9 competing definitions of `.gd-btn-primary`. It renders fine today, but it's a generation artifact that makes every future change a coin-flip.

**Deterministic scan**: detect.mjs returned 5 findings, all false positives for this codebase: 4 `broken-image` hits (index.html:365/560/586/604) are JS-populated image slots that get a real `src` at runtime; 1 `single-font` (Prompt) is correct for a product UI per the product register, where one well-tuned family is the right call.

**Visual overlays**: No browser automation available in this environment, so no live user-visible overlay was produced. Assessment B fell back to the CLI detector plus manual source inspection.

## Overall Impression

The core borrow/return loop is now fast and uncluttered, and the sub-30-second goal in PRODUCT.md is realistic on the QR path. The biggest remaining liability is invisible to users but expensive to the team: the CSS is three outfits worn at once. The biggest user-facing gap is help — a first-time field worker still gets no orientation, and a network error disappears in 3 seconds.

## What's Working

1. **QR-to-borrow shortcut** — scanning a unit QR opens a pre-locked borrow form, the only sub-10-second path, well-placed in both header and borrow tab.
2. **Decluttered borrow flow** — the 4-stat KPI strip moved to the manager dashboard; name pre-filled from auth; employee ID remembered across sessions with numeric keyboard. Real reduction in keystrokes since the last critique.
3. **Accessibility hardening** — numpad keys are now real `<button>` elements, modals carry `role="dialog"`, the toast container is `aria-live="polite"`, focus rings were added, and the PIN lockout offers a "contact admin" path.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] CSS architecture is two design systems in one file**
- **Why it matters**: 897 `!important`, 9 `.gd-btn-primary` definitions, and a "MeetFlow Theme" block layered over "GA Platform Design Tokens" mean every style change risks regressions and the next contributor can't predict which rule wins.
- **Fix**: Pick the GA Platform tokens as canonical, delete the MeetFlow override block, collapse the duplicate component definitions to one each, and strip `!important` once specificity is sane.
- **Suggested command**: /impeccable audit

**[P2] Help and documentation near-absent (heuristic scored 1)**
- **Why it matters**: A first-time field worker (Jordan) gets no orientation — the login heading "ยินดีต้อนรับ" doesn't say what the system does, and there are no tooltips on icon-only controls.
- **Fix**: Add a one-line subtitle on login describing the system ("ระบบยืม-คืนอุปกรณ์"), and contextual hints at the first borrow.
- **Suggested command**: /impeccable onboard

**[P2] White-on-orange hero stats fail contrast**
- **Why it matters**: Hero stat numbers (16px/700) and labels (12px) are white on `#F97316` (~1.7:1), propped up only by a text-shadow. Outdoors in field light these wash out.
- **Fix**: Darken the stat surface chip, or use a darker shade of the orange hue for the label text, to clear 3:1 (large) / 4.5:1 (label).
- **Suggested command**: /impeccable audit

**[P2] Toasts auto-dismiss at 3s with no re-read**
- **Why it matters**: An interrupted, one-handed field user (Casey) loses a network-error message before reading it; there's no way to recall it.
- **Fix**: Make error-tone toasts persist until dismissed (or longer), and keep a recent-notifications affordance.
- **Suggested command**: /impeccable harden

**[P3] Touch-target and label-size inconsistency**
- **Why it matters**: Back button is 44px on the PIN screen but 32px on form overlays and modal close buttons (below the 44px mobile target); header button labels render at 8px and hero labels at 12px, both under the 14px minimum in PRODUCT.md's accessibility note.
- **Fix**: Standardize the back/close control to a single 44px component; raise the smallest labels to 11–12px minimum.
- **Suggested command**: /impeccable adapt
