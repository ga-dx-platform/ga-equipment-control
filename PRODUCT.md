# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two distinct roles:

- **Field/operations staff**: Borrow and return equipment. Primarily mobile, fast task-oriented interactions. They're in a hurry — borrow on the way out, return on the way back. Low tolerance for friction.
- **Managers (mgr role)**: Oversee inventory, review reports, manage recipients. Primarily desktop. Need visibility into who has what and data for reporting.

Both operate inside a Thai-language org context; the UI is in Thai.

## Product Purpose

GA Equipment Control tracks equipment borrowing and returns for an organization. Staff select their name, verify with a PIN, borrow or return gear, and the system keeps inventory accurate and auditable. Managers get a reporting layer.

Success: zero lost or unaccounted equipment; sub-30-second borrow and return flows.

## Brand Personality

Bold, energetic, no-nonsense. The orange brand pushes forward — this is an active operations tool, not a passive record-keeper. Confident, direct, moves fast.

## Anti-references

Not enterprise SaaS: no dark navy + gold, no heavy sidebar-heavy data dashboards, no corporate-stiff UI vocabulary. This is an internal ops tool that should feel like it belongs to the people using it, not to the IT department.

## Design Principles

1. **Action-first**: The primary task (borrow or return) should be reachable in one tap. UI hierarchy reflects task frequency.
2. **Orange owns the room**: The brand color is not a subtle accent — it signals action, ownership, and energy. Use it with confidence.
3. **Hierarchy through contrast**: Important things are dramatically more prominent than supporting things. No flat equal-weight screens.
4. **Speed over decoration**: Motion and effects serve state and feedback, not theatre. The tool disappears into the task.
5. **Trust through legibility**: Dense information stays readable. Thai text at body sizes needs contrast and clear spacing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Thai-language primary; keep text legible at body sizes (14px min, adequate contrast)
- Touch targets appropriate for mobile field use
- WCAG AA contrast minimum for all body text
